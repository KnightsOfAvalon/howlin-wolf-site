What types of guitars did Howlin' Wolf play?
Although Howlin' Wolf was not the main guitarist in his band, he played a number of guitars over the years. Six notable guitars Howlin' Wolf played are:
  • A Kay K-161 ThinTwin
  • A Teisco Tre-100
  • A white Fender Stratocaster
  • A Gibson Firebird V
  • A Fender Coronado
  • A 1965 Epiphone Casino

Did Howlin' Wolf have a family?
Howlin' Wolf was married to a woman named Lillie Handley. The two were deeply in love with each other and they raised two children, Betty and Barbara, together. Betty and Barbara were Lillie's biological daughters from a previous relationship.

What were Howlin' Wolf's first recordings and when were they released?
"How Many More Years" and "Moanin' at Midnight" were the first singles that Howlin' Wolf recorded. They were released in 1951.

What was Howlin' Wolf's first album and when was it released?
Howlin' Wolf's first album was Moanin' in the Moonlight. It included Howlin' Wolf's recordings from 1951 and 1958, including "How Many More Years" and "Moanin' at Midnight".

What was Howlin' Wolf like as a band manager?
Unlike many other bandleaders of his time, Howlin' Wolf had a policy of paying his musicians well and on time. He even included benefits such as unemployment insurance, Social Security contributions, and health insurance. This was largely due to the fact that Howlin' Wolf and his wife managed his professional finances extremely well and they lived comfortably but frugally for the most part. Because of his generous compensation policy for his band, Howlin' Wolf was always able to attract some of the best talent for his band.

Are there any buildings/structures dedicated to Howlin' Wolf?
Yes, there is the Howlin' Wolf Blues Museum located in West Point, Mississippi. It was opened on September 1, 2005.

Where did Howlin' Wolf's birth name, Chester Arthur Burnett, come from?
Chester Arthur Burnett (aka Howlin' Wolf) was named after Chester A. Arthur, the 21st President of the United States.

Howlin' Wolf was known for, among other things, his intimidating physical presence. Just how large was Howlin' Wolf?
